Booking a wheelchair taxi in Bangalore can sometimes feel unpredictable when it comes to pricing. Unlike standard ride-hailing apps, specialized accessible transport involves specialized vehicles, trained drivers, and unique equipment like automatic hydraulic lifts.

In this guide, we break down everything you need to know about wheelchair taxi pricing in Bengaluru.

## Why Do Wheelchair Taxis Cost Different?

Before diving into the numbers, it's essential to understand what you're paying for:

1. **Specialized Equipment**: Our vehicles are equipped with **automatic hydraulic lifts**, ensuring a smooth, manual-handling-free boarding process.
2. **Trained Drivers**: Drivers are trained to securely fasten the wheelchair and assist passengers with mobility challenges.
3. **Vehicle Modifications**: The raised roofs and modified seating arrangements ensure maximum comfort and headroom.

## Typical Pricing Models

### 1. Local City Transfers (Point A to Point B)

For standard hospital visits, social outings, or family events within city limits, pricing is usually based on a base fare plus a per-kilometer rate.

- **Base Fare**: Usually covers the first 5-10 kilometers.
- **Per Kilometer**: Charged for additional distance beyond the base fare.

### 2. Airport Transfers (BIAL Pickup & Drop)

Kempegowda International Airport (BIAL) is located far from the city center. Specialized wheelchair airport taxi services typically offer flat-rate pricing based on your zone in Bangalore.

- **North Bangalore** (Yelahanka, Hebbal): Lower flat rate.
- **Central/East Bangalore** (Indiranagar, MG Road): Medium flat rate.
- **South Bangalore** (Koramangala, Jayanagar, Electronic City): Higher flat rate due to distance and tolls.

### 3. Outstation & Day Packages

If you need the vehicle for an entire day (e.g., attending a wedding, visiting multiple locations) or traveling outstation, day packages are the most economical choice.

- **8 Hours / 80 KM Package**: Ideal for full-day city usage.
- **Outstation**: Billed per kilometer with a minimum driver allowance per day.
